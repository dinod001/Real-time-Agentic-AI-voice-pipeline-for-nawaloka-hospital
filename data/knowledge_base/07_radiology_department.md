## 2. Examination Scheduling & Workflow

### 2.1 Request Process

All imaging requests must be submitted electronically via the HIS (Radiology Order Entry module). Paper requests are accepted only during system downtime.

Required fields: patient HN, clinical indication, relevant history, requesting physician's name and contact extension, urgency level (routine / urgent / STAT).

### 2.2 Urgency Categories

| Category | Target TAT (Report) | Examples |
|----------|---------------------|----------|
| STAT | 1 hour | Acute stroke, trauma, acute abdomen |
| Urgent | 4 hours | Suspected PE, acute appendicitis, bowel obstruction |
| Routine | 24 hours | Follow-up imaging, screening, chronic conditions |

### 2.3 Patient Preparation Guidelines

#### CT Scan

- **With IV Contrast**: NPO 4 hours prior. Serum creatinine / eGFR within the last 30 days. Metformin must be withheld 48 hours before and after contrast. Patients with known contrast allergy receive premedication (Hydrocortisone 200 mg IV + Chlorphenamine 10 mg IV, 1 hour before the scan).
- **Without Contrast**: No special preparation.
- **CT Abdomen/Pelvis**: Oral contrast (Gastrografin 20 mL diluted in 1 L water) to be consumed 1 hour before the scan.

#### MRI

- **MRI Safety Screening**: All patients complete the MRI Safety Questionnaire (Form RAD-MRI-01) before entering Zone III. Absolute contraindications: non-MR-conditional cardiac pacemaker/ICD, metallic foreign body in the orbit, certain cochlear implants, ferromagnetic aneurysm clips.
- **Claustrophobia**: Mild — reassurance, feet-first entry, prism glasses. Moderate/severe — oral sedation (Lorazepam 1 mg PO, 30 min prior) or IV sedation with anaesthetist present.
- **MRI with Gadolinium Contrast**: eGFR must be > 30 mL/min. Patients with eGFR 30–60 require nephrologist clearance. Gadolinium is contraindicated if eGFR < 30 (risk of nephrogenic systemic fibrosis).

#### Mammography

- Schedule preferably during days 7–14 of the menstrual cycle (reduced breast tenderness).
- No deodorant, talcum powder, or body lotion on the day.
- Bring prior mammograms for comparison.

#### Ultrasound

- **Abdominal US**: NPO 6 hours.
- **Pelvic US (transabdominal)**: Full bladder (drink 1 L water, 1 hour before).
- **Renal / Thyroid / MSK US**: No preparation.

## 3. Contrast Agent Protocols

### 3.1 Iodinated Contrast (CT)

- Standard agent: Iohexol (Omnipaque 350) or Iopamidol (Isovue 370).
- Standard dose: 1.5 mL/kg body weight, max 150 mL.
- Injection rate: 3–4 mL/sec via power injector through an 18–20G IV cannula in the antecubital fossa.
- Post-injection monitoring: 30 minutes in the department for allergic reactions. Resuscitation trolley immediately available.

### 3.2 Gadolinium Contrast (MRI)

- Standard agent: Gadobutrol (Gadovist 1.0 mmol/mL).
- Standard dose: 0.1 mmol/kg (0.1 mL/kg).
- Injection: Manual push via 22G IV cannula, followed by 20 mL N/S flush.

### 3.3 Contrast Reaction Management

| Severity | Symptoms | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Mild | Urticaria, itching, nausea | Observe; Chlorphenamine 10 mg IV if needed |
| Moderate | Bronchospasm, facial oedema, tachycardia | Adrenaline 0.3 mg IM (mid-outer thigh); Hydrocortisone 200 mg IV; Salbutamol nebuliser |
| Severe (Anaphylaxis) | Hypotension, laryngeal oedema, cardiac arrest | Call Code Blue; Adrenaline 0.5 mg IM (repeat q 5 min); IV fluids; intubation if needed |

## 4. Radiation Safety

### 4.1 ALARA Principle

All imaging involving ionising radiation follows the **ALARA (As Low As Reasonably Achievable)** principle:
- Use the lowest effective dose.
- Collimate tightly to the area of interest.
- Shield radiosensitive organs (thyroid, gonads) when not in the primary beam.
- Avoid repeat exposures — check HIS for recent imaging before ordering.

### 4.2 Staff Dosimetry

All staff working with ionising radiation wear TLD (Thermoluminescent Dosimeter) badges, exchanged monthly. The Radiation Safety Officer (RSO) reviews dose reports and investigates any readings exceeding 1 mSv/month.

### 4.3 Pregnancy Policy

Pregnant staff are reassigned to non-ionising-radiation duties (ultrasound, MRI, admin) for the duration of the pregnancy. Pregnant patients requiring X-ray or CT must have the benefit/risk discussed and documented; lead shielding of the abdomen is mandatory.

## 5. Interventional Radiology

The IR Suite (Suite E) performs the following procedures:
- **Vascular**: Angiography, angioplasty, stenting, embolisation, IVC filter placement, PICC line insertion, permcath insertion.
- **Non-Vascular**: Image-guided biopsy (liver, lung, kidney), abscess drainage, nephrostomy, biliary drainage, radiofrequency ablation (RFA) of liver and renal tumours.

Pre-procedure requirements: informed consent, coagulation profile (INR < 1.5, platelets > 50,000), IV access, NPO 6 hours. Post-procedure: 4-hour observation in the Day Surgery Unit; overnight admission for complex cases.

## 6. Reporting & Communication

### 6.1 Report Turnaround Times

Radiologists dictate reports using voice-recognition software (Nuance PowerScribe). Reports are verified and signed electronically. TAT targets are monitored on the departmental dashboard.

### 6.2 Critical Findings Communication

Critical or unexpected findings require **direct verbal communication** with the referring physician within 30 minutes:
- Aortic dissection, pulmonary embolism, acute stroke, pneumothorax, ectopic pregnancy, new malignancy.
- The radiologist documents the communication (date, time, name of person notified) in the report footer.
