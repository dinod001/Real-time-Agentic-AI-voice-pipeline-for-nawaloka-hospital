# Nawaloka Hospital — Billing, Insurance & Payment Policies

## 1. Fee Schedule Overview

Nawaloka Hospitals PLC operates a transparent fee schedule reviewed annually by the Finance Committee. All charges are listed in the Hospital Tariff Book (available at the Billing Counter and on the internal staff portal).

### 1.1 Consultation Fees

| Category | Fee Range (LKR) | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Consultant Channelling (OPD) | 2,500–5,000 | Varies by specialty and consultant seniority |
| Follow-up Visit (within 7 days) | 1,500–3,000 | Must be with the same consultant |
| Emergency Consultation | 3,500 | Flat fee; does not include investigation charges |
| Telemedicine Consultation | 2,000 | Available via the MyNawaloka app |
| Second Opinion | 4,000 | Cross-referral between departments |

### 1.2 Room Charges (Per Day)

| Room Type | Rate (LKR/day) | Includes |
|-----------|----------------|----------|

| Private Room | 20,000 | Above + air conditioning, mini-fridge, guest bed |

| ICU / CCU | 45,000 | Continuous monitoring, 1:2 nursing ratio, ventilator support |

## 2. Payment Methods

### 2.1 Accepted Payment Modes

| Mode | Details |
|------|---------|
| Cash | LKR only; foreign currency accepted at the International Patient Counter |
| Credit / Debit Card | Visa, Mastercard, Amex, UnionPay; minimum LKR 500 |
| Online Transfer | Bank transfers to Nawaloka Hospitals PLC — Bank of Ceylon account; provide HN as reference |
| Mobile Payment | FriMi, genie (Dialog), eZ Cash — available at Billing Counters 1–4 |
| Corporate Guarantee | Pre-approved corporate clients (see Section 3.2) |
| Insurance | Direct billing for panel insurers (see Section 3.1) |

### 2.2 Advance Deposits

An advance deposit is collected at admission:
- General Ward: LKR 25,000
- Semi-Private / Private: LKR 50,000
- Deluxe Suite: LKR 75,000
- ICU / CCU: LKR 100,000
- Surgical Admissions: Estimated procedure cost × 50%

Deposits are refundable if the admission is cancelled before occupying the bed.

### 2.3 Interim Billing

For stays exceeding 3 days, an interim bill is issued every 72 hours. Patients or guarantors are expected to settle interim bills within 24 hours to avoid service holds (non-emergency services may be paused).

## 3. Insurance & Corporate Partners

### 3.1 Panel Insurance Companies

Nawaloka has direct billing agreements with the following insurers:

- Sri Lanka Insurance Corporation (SLIC)
- Ceylinco General Insurance
- AIA Insurance Lanka
- Allianz Insurance Lanka
- Fairfirst Insurance
- HNB Assurance
- Union Assurance
- Continental Insurance Lanka
- Janashakthi Insurance

For panel insurers, the patient presents their insurance card and NIC at admission. The hospital obtains pre-authorisation electronically. The insured portion is billed directly to the insurer; the patient pays only the excess / co-payment.

### 3.2 Corporate Accounts

Nawaloka maintains corporate health agreements with over 150 companies. Employees of corporate partners present their company letter or corporate health card. Billing is routed to the company's HR/Finance department on a monthly settlement cycle (net 30 days).

### 3.3 Non-Panel Insurance

For insurers without a direct billing agreement, the patient pays the full bill and claims reimbursement from their insurer. The hospital provides a detailed itemised bill, discharge summary, and investigation reports for the claim.

## 4. Billing Disputes & Adjustments

### 4.1 Dispute Resolution Process

1. The patient raises the concern at the Billing Help Desk (Counter 10, Ground Floor).
2. The Billing Supervisor reviews the itemised bill against the clinical records within 24 hours.
3. If an error is found, a credit note is issued and the adjusted bill re-presented.
4. If no error is found, the Billing Supervisor provides a written explanation to the patient.
5. Unresolved disputes are escalated to the Finance Manager, who must respond within 5 working days.

### 4.2 Charity & Financial Hardship

Patients demonstrating financial hardship may apply for a bill concession through the Nawaloka Welfare Fund:
1. Complete the Financial Hardship Application (Form FIN-03).
2. Provide supporting documentation (Grama Niladhari letter, income certificate).
3. The Welfare Committee reviews and approves partial or full waivers.
4. The maximum waiver per case is LKR 500,000 (higher amounts require Board approval).

## 5. Refund Policy

- OPD consultations: Non-refundable once the consultation has commenced.
- Investigations: Refundable if cancelled before the procedure. 50% refund if cancelled after preparation (contrast, sedation).
- Admission deposits: Full refund if the admission does not proceed.
- Overpayments: Refunded within 14 working days via cheque or bank transfer.

## 6. Medical Tourism Pricing

International patients are billed under the **Nawaloka International Patient Programme**:
- All charges are quoted in USD at the prevailing exchange rate.
- A **Package Pricing** option is available for common procedures (e.g., cardiac check-up package: USD 350; knee replacement package: USD 5,500).
- Packages include: consultation, investigations, procedure, room charges (up to a specified number of days), and airport transfer.
- Visa support letters and medical reports for travel insurance claims are provided by the International Patient Coordinator (ext. 1300).
