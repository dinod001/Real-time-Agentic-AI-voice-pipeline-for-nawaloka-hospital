# Nawaloka Hospital — Dermatology Department Handbook

## 1. Department Overview

The Nawaloka Dermatology Department provides specialist care for conditions of the skin, hair, and nails for both adults and children. Consultations are run as outpatient channelling (specialist consultant) clinics, by appointment. Walk-in patients with non-urgent skin concerns are seen through the General OPD and referred to dermatology where needed; urgent skin presentations (severe drug reactions, widespread blistering, suspected skin infection with fever) are seen through the Emergency Department, which operates 24 hours.

The department's consultant dermatologists hold channelling clinics during the specialist consultant clinic band (afternoon and evening, by appointment). The current list of available dermatology consultants and their booking slots is held in the hospital's appointment system — ask the assistant to "show me the dermatology doctors" or call the main line on +94 11 544 4444, and the assistant can book, reschedule, or cancel an appointment for you.

## 2. Conditions Treated

The dermatology team manages, among others:

- **Acne and acne scarring** — medical management and procedural treatment.
- **Eczema and dermatitis** — atopic, contact, and seborrhoeic dermatitis.
- **Psoriasis** — topical therapy, phototherapy referral, and systemic management.
- **Fungal, bacterial, and viral skin infections** — including tinea, cellulitis follow-up, and warts.
- **Skin allergies and urticaria (hives)** — including patch testing referral.
- **Pigmentation disorders** — melasma, vitiligo, post-inflammatory pigmentation.
- **Hair and scalp disorders** — hair loss (alopecia), dandruff, scalp infections.
- **Nail disorders** — fungal nails, ingrown nails, nail dystrophy.
- **Skin cancer screening** — mole checks, dermoscopy, and biopsy of suspicious lesions.

## 3. Services and Procedures

- **Consultation and diagnosis** — history, skin examination, and dermoscopy where indicated.
- **Skin biopsy** — for diagnosis of rashes, tumours, and suspicious lesions; report typically available within a few working days.
- **Minor dermatological surgery** — removal of cysts, skin tags, warts, and small skin lesions (dermal procedures).
- **Cryotherapy** — freezing treatment for warts and certain benign lesions.
- **Cosmetic dermatology** — clinically indicated treatments for scarring and pigmentation.
- **Patch testing** — to identify contact allergens (booked separately).

## 4. Preparing for a Dermatology Appointment

- Bring a list of any creams, ointments, or medications you are currently using (including over-the-counter products).
- If you have photographs of a rash on a good day or a bad day, bring them — skin conditions change over time.
- Avoid wearing makeup or nail polish if your appointment concerns the face or nails.
- Bring any previous biopsy or test reports relevant to the skin problem.

## 5. Booking, Rescheduling, and Cancelling

Dermatology appointments are managed through the same appointment system as the rest of the hospital. You can:

- **See available doctors** — ask for the dermatology consultants and the assistant will list them with contact numbers.
- **Book** — give a doctor and a date and time (for example, "book Dr. Nirmala Fernando tomorrow at 11 am").
- **Reschedule** — ask to move an existing appointment to a new time.
- **Cancel** — ask to cancel your dermatology appointment.

If you are unsure which dermatologist to see, the assistant or the OPD desk can help you choose based on availability.
