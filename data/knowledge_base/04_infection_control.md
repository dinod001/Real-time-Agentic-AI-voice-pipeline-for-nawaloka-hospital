# Nawaloka Hospital — Infection Prevention & Control Manual

## 1. Hand Hygiene Policy

Hand hygiene is the single most effective measure to prevent healthcare-associated infections (HAIs). Nawaloka follows the **WHO "5 Moments for Hand Hygiene"** framework:

1. **Before patient contact.**
2. **Before an aseptic procedure** (e.g., IV insertion, wound dressing).
3. **After body-fluid exposure risk** (e.g., handling specimens, emptying drains).
4. **After patient contact.**
5. **After contact with patient surroundings** (bed rails, monitors, IV poles).

### 1.1 Methods

| Method | Duration | When to Use |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Alcohol-based hand rub (ABHR) | 20–30 sec | Routine clinical contact; hands not visibly soiled |
| Soap and water | 40–60 sec | Visibly soiled hands; after contact with C. difficile or norovirus patients; after using the toilet |
| Surgical hand scrub | 3–5 min | Before all surgical procedures |

ABHR dispensers are mounted at every room entrance, nursing station, and corridor junction. Soap dispensers are available in every patient room and all restrooms.

### 1.2 Compliance Monitoring

The Infection Control Team (ICT) conducts **covert hand-hygiene audits** monthly. The hospital target is ≥ 85% compliance. Results are displayed on the ward Hand Hygiene Dashboard and discussed at the monthly Infection Control Committee meeting.

## 2. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

### 2.1 Standard Precautions

Standard precautions apply to **all patients regardless of diagnosis**:

- **Gloves**: For contact with blood, body fluids, mucous membranes, and non-intact skin.
- **Gown / Apron**: When splashing or soiling is anticipated.
- **Eye Protection / Face Shield**: For procedures generating splashes or aerosols.
- **Surgical Mask**: For droplet-generating procedures at the bedside.

### 2.2 Transmission-Based Precautions

| Precaution Type | Trigger Conditions | Additional PPE | Room Requirement |
|----------------|-------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Contact | MRSA, VRE, C. difficile, scabies | Gown + gloves for all room entry | Single room preferred; cohort if unavailable |
| Droplet | Influenza, pertussis, meningococcal disease | Surgical mask within 1 m of patient | Single room; door may be open |
| Airborne | TB (pulmonary/laryngeal), measles, varicella, COVID-19 | N95 respirator (fit-tested), eye protection | Negative-pressure isolation room (NPIR) |

Nawaloka has **8 negative-pressure isolation rooms**: 4 in ICU Block A, 2 in the Infectious Disease ward (4th Floor), and 2 in the ED Isolation Unit.

### 2.3 PPE Donning and Doffing

All clinical staff must follow the posted donning/doffing sequence charts (displayed outside every isolation room). Donning order: hand hygiene → gown → mask/respirator → eye protection → gloves. Doffing order: gloves → eye protection → gown → mask/respirator → hand hygiene.

## 3. Environmental Cleaning & Disinfection

### 3.1 Routine Cleaning Schedule

| Area | Frequency | Agent |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Patient rooms (occupied) | Twice daily + after discharge | Quaternary ammonium compound (QAC) |
| High-touch surfaces (bed rails, call bells, door handles) | Every 4 hours | QAC or 70% isopropyl alcohol wipes |
| Operating theatres | Between cases + terminal clean at end of day | Sodium hypochlorite 1,000 ppm |
| ICU bed spaces | Every shift + terminal clean on patient transfer | Sodium hypochlorite 1,000 ppm |
| Public areas (lobbies, lifts, restrooms) | Every 2 hours | QAC |

### 3.2 Terminal Cleaning

A terminal clean is performed after every patient discharge or transfer. It includes cleaning of all surfaces, removal and laundering of curtains, disinfection of mattress and pillow covers, and replacement of disposable items.

For rooms that housed patients with multi-drug-resistant organisms (MDROs) or C. difficile, a **hydrogen peroxide vapour (HPV) decontamination** cycle is performed after terminal cleaning, before the room is reassigned.

### 3.3 Spill Management

Blood and body-fluid spills must be managed immediately:
1. Restrict the area (place a wet-floor sign).
2. Don gloves and apron.
3. Cover the spill with absorbent granules (Vernacare or equivalent).
4. Scoop into a yellow clinical-waste bag.
5. Clean the area with sodium hypochlorite 10,000 ppm, leave for 3 minutes, then wipe dry.
6. Dispose of all materials in the clinical waste stream.

## 4. Sterilisation & Reprocessing

### 4.1 Central Sterile Supply Department (CSSD)

All reusable surgical instruments are processed in the CSSD (Basement Level 1):

1. **Collection**: Used instruments are transported in sealed containers via the dedicated dirty lift.
2. **Decontamination**: Automated washer-disinfector (AWD) at 93°C for 1 minute or enzymatic soak + ultrasonic cleaning for delicate instruments.
3. **Inspection & Assembly**: Each instrument is inspected under magnification, assembled into trays per the standardised tray lists, and wrapped in SMS (spunbond-meltblown-spunbond) sterilisation wrap.
4. **Sterilisation**: Pre-vacuum steam autoclave at 134°C for 3.5 minutes (standard cycle) or 18 minutes at 121°C (for heat-sensitive items).
5. **Storage**: Sterile packs are stored in the clean store on ventilated shelving. Shelf life: 6 months for double-wrapped packs; 30 days for single-wrapped.

### 4.2 Biological Monitoring

Biological indicators (Geobacillus stearothermophilus spore strips) are run with every autoclave load. Results are read at 24 and 48 hours. A positive result triggers an immediate recall of all items from that load.

## 5. Surveillance & Reporting

### 5.1 HAI Surveillance

The ICT conducts prospective surveillance for:
- Surgical site infections (SSIs) — tracked for 30 days post-operatively (90 days for implant surgery).
- Central line-associated bloodstream infections (CLABSIs).
- Catheter-associated urinary tract infections (CAUTIs).
- Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP).

Monthly HAI rates are benchmarked against the Sri Lanka College of Microbiologists' national data.

### 5.2 Outbreak Management

An outbreak is declared when ≥ 2 cases of the same infection are epidemiologically linked within 14 days. The ICT convenes the Outbreak Management Team (OMT) within 4 hours of declaration. Actions include enhanced screening, patient cohorting, environmental sampling, and communication to the Ministry of Health if a notifiable disease is involved.

### 5.3 Antibiotic Stewardship

Nawaloka operates an Antibiotic Stewardship Programme (ASP) to combat antimicrobial resistance:
- Restricted antibiotics (carbapenems, colistin, linezolid, daptomycin) require ASP approval before dispensing.
- Empiric antibiotic therapy is guided by the Nawaloka Antibiogram, updated annually.
- The ASP pharmacist reviews all antibiotic orders at 48 hours and provides de-escalation recommendations.

## 6. Sharps & Needlestick Injury Management

### 6.1 Safe Sharps Handling

- Use safety-engineered devices (retractable needles, needleless IV connectors) wherever available.
- Never recap, bend, or break needles.
- Dispose of sharps immediately into the yellow sharps container at the point of use. Containers must not be filled beyond the ¾ line.

### 6.2 Post-Exposure Protocol

If a needlestick or sharps injury occurs:
1. Wash the wound immediately with soap and running water (do not squeeze).
2. Report to the Occupational Health Unit (OHU) within 1 hour.
3. The OHU assesses the source patient (with consent) for HIV, Hepatitis B, and Hepatitis C.
4. Post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) is initiated within 2 hours if indicated.
5. Follow-up serology at 6 weeks, 3 months, and 6 months.
