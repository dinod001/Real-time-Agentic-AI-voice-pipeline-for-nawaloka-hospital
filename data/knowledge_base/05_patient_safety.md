# Nawaloka Hospital — Patient Safety & Quality Improvement

## 1. Patient Identification

### 1.1 Two-Identifier Rule

Before any clinical intervention (medication, blood draw, procedure, transfer), staff must verify the patient's identity using **two independent identifiers**:

1. **Full name** (as printed on the wrist band).
2. **Hospital Number (HN)** (barcode on the wrist band).

Asking "What is your name?" is preferred over "Are you Mr. Perera?" to avoid confirmation bias.

### 1.2 Wrist Band Colour Codes

| Colour | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| White | Standard patient |
| Red | Known drug allergy |
| Yellow | Fall risk |
| Purple | Do Not Resuscitate (DNR) |
| Pink | Restricted extremity (no BP / IV on that arm) |

Wrist bands are applied at admission and remain on until discharge. If a band is removed (e.g., for a procedure), it must be re-applied immediately and verified by two staff members.

## 2. Medication Safety

### 2.1 High-Alert Medications

The following categories are designated high-alert at Nawaloka:

- **Insulins** — all formulations.
- **Anticoagulants** — heparin, warfarin, enoxaparin, rivaroxaban.
- **Concentrated electrolytes** — potassium chloride > 20 mEq/L, hypertonic saline (3% NaCl), magnesium sulphate 50%.
- **Opioids** — morphine, fentanyl, pethidine.
- **Chemotherapy agents** — all cytotoxic drugs.
- **Neuromuscular blocking agents** — suxamethonium, rocuronium.

High-alert medications are stored separately with prominent **"HIGH ALERT"** labels. Independent double-checking is mandatory before administration.

### 2.2 Look-Alike Sound-Alike (LASA) Medications

A LASA list is maintained by the Pharmacy Department and updated quarterly. Examples:

| Medication A | Medication B | Differentiator |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Losartan | Lisinopril | Tall-man lettering: LOSartan vs lisinoPRIL |
| Metformin | Metoprolol | Shelf separation + coloured labels |
| Prednisolone | Prednisone | Tall-man lettering: predniSOLONE vs predniSONE |

### 2.3 Medication Error Reporting

All medication errors and near-misses must be reported via the electronic **Safety Reporting System (SRS)** within 12 hours. Reports are non-punitive and confidential. The Medication Safety Committee reviews all Category D–I errors (those reaching the patient) within 48 hours and implements corrective actions.

### 2.4 Medication Reconciliation

Medication reconciliation is performed at four transition points:
1. **Admission** — current home medications documented in the MAR.
2. **Transfer** between wards or to/from ICU.
3. **Post-operative** — anaesthesia and surgical medications reconciled.
4. **Discharge** — discharge medications compared against the admission list; discrepancies resolved with the consultant.

## 3. Fall Prevention Programme

### 3.1 Risk Assessment

Every patient is assessed on admission using the **Morse Fall Scale (MFS)**:

| Score | Risk Level | Interventions |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 0–24 | Low | Standard fall precautions |
| 25–44 | Moderate | Yellow wrist band, bed alarm, non-slip footwear provided |
| ≥ 45 | High | All moderate interventions + 1:1 attendant, bed in lowest position, hourly rounding |

Reassessment occurs every shift, after a fall, after a change in condition, and before transfer.

### 3.2 Environmental Measures

- Wet-floor signs placed immediately after mopping.
- Night lights in all patient rooms and corridors.
- Handrails on both sides of all corridors and bathrooms.
- Call bells within reach of the patient at all times.
- Clutter-free pathways checked during hourly rounding.

### 3.3 Post-Fall Protocol

1. **Do not move the patient** until assessed for head, neck, or spinal injury.
2. Call the attending physician for examination.
3. Document the fall in the SRS within 1 hour.
4. Reassess MFS and upgrade interventions.
5. Notify the patient's family within 2 hours.
6. The Quality & Safety team conducts a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) for falls resulting in moderate or serious injury.

## 4. Pressure Injury Prevention

### 4.1 Braden Scale Assessment

All immobile or critically ill patients are assessed using the **Braden Scale** on admission and every 48 hours thereafter.

| Score | Risk Level | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| 19–23 | Not at risk | Standard care |
| 15–18 | Mild risk | Pressure-redistribution mattress, 2-hourly repositioning |
| 13–14 | Moderate risk | Above + skin barrier cream, nutritional assessment |
| 10–12 | High risk | Above + air-fluidised mattress, wound-care consult |
| ≤ 9 | Very high risk | Above + 1-hourly repositioning, offloading devices |

### 4.2 Skin Inspection

A head-to-toe skin inspection is performed on admission and at every shift. Any new redness, blanching, or skin breakdown is documented with a photograph in the electronic wound-care record.

## 5. Incident Reporting & Investigation

### 5.1 What to Report

All adverse events, near-misses, and unsafe conditions must be reported:

- Patient safety incidents (falls, medication errors, wrong-site procedures).
- Staff injuries (needlestick, slips, manual-handling injuries).
- Equipment failures or malfunctions.
- Security incidents (aggression, theft, abscondment).
- Environmental hazards (spills, broken fixtures, pest sightings).

### 5.2 Reporting Process

1. Complete the online SRS form within 12 hours of the event.
2. The reporter's identity is kept confidential.
3. The Quality & Safety team triages reports using a **Severity Assessment Code (SAC)** matrix (likelihood × consequence).
4. SAC 1 (extreme) events trigger a formal **Root Cause Analysis (RCA)** within 7 days.
5. SAC 2 (high) events require a **Concise Investigation** within 14 days.
6. SAC 3–4 events are aggregated and reviewed at the monthly Quality Committee meeting.

### 5.3 Learning from Incidents

Key findings and recommendations from investigations are communicated through:
- **Safety Alerts** — emailed to all clinical staff.
- **Lessons Learned Boards** — displayed in each ward's staff area.
- **Grand Rounds presentations** — quarterly, featuring de-identified case studies.

## 6. Clinical Audit Programme

The hospital conducts a rolling programme of clinical audits aligned with national and international standards:

- Hand-hygiene compliance (monthly).
- Surgical Safety Checklist compliance (quarterly).
- VTE prophylaxis adherence (quarterly).
- Antibiotic prescribing audit (quarterly).
- Pain management audit (bi-annually).
- Patient experience survey (bi-annually).

Audit results are reported to the Board Quality Committee and drive the annual Quality Improvement Plan.
