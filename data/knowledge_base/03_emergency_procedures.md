# Nawaloka Hospital — Emergency Response Procedures

## 1. Code Blue — Cardiac / Respiratory Arrest

### 1.1 Activation

Any staff member who discovers an unresponsive patient should:

1. Call for help and press the **Code Blue button** (located at every bedside and nursing station).
2. Dial **extension 1111** and state: "Code Blue, [location], [patient name if known]".
3. Begin **CPR immediately** — 30 compressions : 2 breaths (adult), rate 100–120/min, depth 5–6 cm.
4. Retrieve the nearest **AED (Automated External Defibrillator)** — wall-mounted in every ward corridor, ED, ICU entrance, lobbies on each floor, and the staff canteen.

### 1.2 Code Blue Team

The Code Blue Team assembles within **3 minutes** of activation:

| Role | Responder | Responsibility |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| Team Leader | On-call ICU Consultant / Senior MO | Directs resuscitation, makes clinical decisions |
| Airway Manager | Anaesthetist on-call | Intubation, ventilation, oxygen management |
| Chest Compressor 1 | First responder (rotates q 2 min) | High-quality compressions |
| Chest Compressor 2 | Ward nurse / MO | Relief compressor |
| IV/Medication Nurse | ICU nurse | Establishes IV access, administers medications per ACLS |
| Recorder | Ward clerk / junior nurse | Documents timeline, medications, rhythm checks |
| Runner | Any available staff | Fetches equipment, blood gases, calls family |

### 1.3 Post-Resuscitation

- If ROSC (Return of Spontaneous Circulation) achieved → transfer to ICU/CCU.
- If resuscitation unsuccessful → Team Leader documents time of death, notifies family, completes the Death Certificate process.
- A **debrief session** is held within 1 hour for the responding team.
- The Code Blue Audit Form (Form CB-01) is submitted to the Clinical Governance Office within 24 hours.

## 2. Code Red — Fire Emergency

### 2.1 RACE Protocol

All staff must follow the **RACE** protocol upon discovering a fire:

- **R — Rescue**: Remove patients and visitors from the immediate danger zone.
- **A — Alarm**: Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station (red boxes at corridor intersections).
- **C — Contain**: Close all doors and windows to limit fire spread.
- **E — Extinguish / Evacuate**: Use the appropriate fire extinguisher if safe to do so; otherwise evacuate.

### 2.2 Fire Extinguisher Types

| Type | Label Colour | Use For |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Water | Red | Paper, wood, fabric (Class A) |
| CO₂ | Black | Electrical fires (Class E) |
| Dry Chemical | Blue | Flammable liquids, gases (Class B/C) |
| Wet Chemical | Yellow | Kitchen / cooking oil fires (Class F) |

**Never use a water extinguisher on electrical or chemical fires.**

### 2.3 Evacuation Routes

Each floor has two designated evacuation staircases (Staircase A — east wing, Staircase B — west wing). Lifts must **not** be used during fire evacuations. Assembly points:

- **Point 1**: Main car park (front of hospital, opposite Beira Lake side).
- **Point 2**: Rear car park (behind the Pharmacy building).
- **Point 3**: Ambulance bay (for non-ambulatory patients on stretchers).

Ward fire wardens (identified by orange vests) conduct headcounts at the assembly point and report to the Fire Safety Officer.

### 2.4 Fire Drills

Unannounced fire drills are conducted quarterly. Each department must achieve full evacuation of simulated patients within **8 minutes** for upper floors and **5 minutes** for ground-floor departments.

## 3. Code Orange — Mass Casualty Incident (MCI)

### 3.1 Activation

Activated by the Duty Manager or ED Consultant when the number of casualties exceeds the ED's normal capacity (typically > 10 patients simultaneously).

### 3.2 Response Actions

1. The **Hospital Command Centre** (Board Room, 6th Floor) is activated.
2. Off-duty staff are recalled via the Emergency SMS System.
3. Elective surgeries are postponed; operating theatres 3 and 4 are reserved for MCI cases.
4. The ED is divided into **Triage Zone** (entrance), **Red Zone** (critical), **Yellow Zone** (urgent), and **Green Zone** (walking wounded).
5. The Hospital Chaplain and Psychosocial Team are alerted for family support.
6. The Media Liaison Officer handles all press enquiries from the designated briefing room (Ground Floor Conference Room B).

### 3.3 Deactivation

The MCI is deactivated by the Incident Commander once all patients are triaged, treated, or transferred. A formal After-Action Review (AAR) is conducted within 72 hours.

## 4. Code Pink — Infant / Child Abduction

1. The ward nurse activates the alarm by dialling **extension 1113** with the description of the child and suspected abductor.
2. All exits are secured by Security within **2 minutes** (magnetic door locks engage automatically).
3. CCTV operators scan all exit footage in real-time.
4. The Police (119) are notified simultaneously by the Security Control Room.
5. No one leaves the premises until cleared by the Incident Commander.

## 5. Code Grey — Aggressive / Violent Person

1. Staff should not confront the individual. Dial **extension 1114** for the Security Response Team.
2. Remove other patients and visitors from the area calmly.
3. The Psychiatric Liaison Team is called if the person appears to be in a mental health crisis.
4. Physical restraint is a **last resort** and may only be applied by trained security personnel using approved soft restraints. Chemical restraint requires a physician's order.

## 6. Utility Failure Procedures

### 6.1 Power Failure

- The UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) provides 10 seconds of bridge power for critical systems.
- Diesel generators auto-start within 15 seconds, powering: ICU, CCU, operating theatres, NICU, ED, blood bank, lift #1, and emergency lighting.
- Non-critical departments (admin offices, outpatient clinics) may remain without power until mains are restored.
- The Biomedical Engineering team checks all life-support equipment within 5 minutes of a power event.

### 6.2 Water Supply Failure

- The hospital maintains a 48-hour water reserve in rooftop tanks (capacity: 200,000 litres).
- Bottled water is distributed to wards from the emergency stockpile (Basement Store B).
- The Facilities Manager coordinates with the National Water Supply & Drainage Board for restoration.

### 6.3 Medical Gas Failure (Oxygen / Nitrous Oxide)

- Affected wards switch to portable oxygen cylinders (E-type cylinders stored in each ward utility room).
- The Biomedical Engineering team isolates the faulty manifold and activates the reserve manifold.
- ICU and theatre ventilators have built-in oxygen concentrators as backup.
