# Nawaloka Hospital — Staff Handbook & HR Policies

## 1. Introduction

Welcome to Nawaloka Hospitals PLC. This handbook outlines the policies, procedures, and expectations that apply to every employee, from clinical staff to administrative personnel. By joining Nawaloka you agree to uphold the standards described herein. The hospital reserves the right to amend these policies at any time; amendments are communicated via the internal HR portal and departmental notice boards.

## 2. Employment Categories

Nawaloka recognises the following employment categories:

- **Permanent Full-Time** — Contracted for 45 hours per week with full benefits (EPF, ETF, medical cover, annual bonus).
- **Permanent Part-Time** — Contracted for 20–30 hours per week with pro-rated benefits.
- **Probationary** — New permanent hires serve a 6-month probation period with quarterly reviews.
- **Contract / Locum** — Fixed-term engagement (3–12 months) with a separate benefits schedule.
- **Intern / Trainee** — Medical officers, nursing trainees, and allied-health students rotating through accredited programmes.

## 3. Working Hours & Shifts

### 3.1 Standard Shifts

| Shift | Start | End | Duration |
|-------|-------|-----|----------|
| Morning | 06:00 | 14:00 | 8 h |
| Afternoon | 14:00 | 22:00 | 8 h |
| Night | 22:00 | 06:00 | 8 h |
| Admin Day | 08:30 | 17:00 | 8.5 h |

A 30-minute paid meal break is included in every clinical shift. Administrative staff receive a 60-minute unpaid lunch break.

### 3.2 Overtime

Overtime applies when actual hours exceed 45 per week. Rates are 1.5× for weekdays and 2× for Sundays and public holidays. Overtime must be pre-approved by the department head.

### 3.3 On-Call Duty

Consultants and senior medical officers participate in on-call rosters. On-call allowance is LKR 5,000 per 12-hour block. If called in, the time worked is compensated at the overtime rate.

## 4. Leave Entitlements

### 4.1 Annual Leave

- Permanent full-time: 14 working days per annum.
- Permanent part-time: Pro-rated.
- Leave accrues monthly (1.17 days/month) and unused leave can be carried forward up to a maximum of 7 days into the next calendar year.

### 4.2 Sick Leave

- 21 days per annum with pay.
- A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 2 consecutive days.
- The Occupational Health Unit may request an independent medical examination for extended or frequent sick leave.

### 4.3 Maternity & Paternity Leave

- Maternity: 84 working days (first two children), 42 working days for subsequent children, in line with the Sri Lankan Shop and Office Employees Act.
- Paternity: 3 working days within 14 days of the birth.

### 4.4 Other Leave Types

- **Compassionate Leave:** 5 days for death of a close family member.
- **Exam Leave:** Up to 10 days for professional examinations (e.g., MD, MRCP), with HOD approval.
- **No-Pay Leave:** May be granted at the discretion of the Director-HR, up to 30 days per year.

## 5. Code of Conduct

### 5.1 Professional Behaviour

All employees must:
- Treat patients, visitors, and colleagues with courtesy and respect.
- Maintain patient confidentiality at all times (refer to the Data Privacy & Security policy).
- Report any conflict of interest to the Compliance Officer.
- Refrain from accepting gifts or hospitality from pharmaceutical or medical-device companies exceeding LKR 2,500 per instance.

### 5.2 Dress Code

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Medical Officers | White coat over smart-casual attire; name badge visible. |
| Nursing Staff | Approved uniform (white or department-colour scrubs); closed-toe shoes. |
| Surgical / Theatre | Hospital-issued scrubs; no jewellery below the elbow. |
| Admin / Front Desk | Smart business attire; name badge visible. |
| Allied Health | Department-specific uniform or lab coat. |

### 5.3 Use of Mobile Phones

Personal mobile phones must be on silent mode in all patient-care areas. Use of personal phones for photography is strictly prohibited in clinical zones.

## 6. Disciplinary Process

Nawaloka follows a progressive discipline model:

1. **Verbal Warning** — documented in the employee's file.
2. **First Written Warning** — valid for 6 months.
3. **Final Written Warning** — valid for 12 months.
4. **Suspension / Termination** — with or without pay, depending on severity.

Gross misconduct (theft, patient abuse, intoxication on duty, falsification of records) may lead to immediate termination without prior warnings.

## 7. Grievance Procedure

Employees may raise grievances through the following channels:
1. Immediate supervisor (within 5 working days of the incident).
2. Department Head (if unresolved within 10 working days).
3. HR Grievance Committee (formal hearing within 15 working days).
4. Director-General (final appeal within 5 working days of the committee decision).

All grievance records are kept confidential. Retaliation against employees who raise grievances is a terminable offence.

## 8. Performance Appraisal

Performance reviews are conducted bi-annually (January and July). The appraisal framework evaluates:
- Clinical / functional competence (40%)
- Patient / stakeholder feedback (20%)
- Teamwork and communication (15%)
- Adherence to policies and procedures (15%)
- Professional development and initiative (10%)

Outstanding performers are eligible for accelerated increments, training sponsorships, and recognition at the annual Staff Awards Ceremony held each December.

## 9. Employee Benefits

### 9.1 Medical Cover

All permanent employees and their dependants (spouse + up to 2 children under 21) receive free outpatient and inpatient care at Nawaloka. Dental and optical services are covered up to LKR 50,000 per annum.

### 9.2 EPF & ETF

Employer contributions: EPF 12%, ETF 3%, deducted and remitted monthly in compliance with the Employees' Provident Fund Act No. 15 of 1958.

### 9.3 Staff Canteen

Subsidised meals are available at the 3rd-floor staff canteen from 06:00 to 22:00. Night-shift staff may pre-order meals via the internal portal.

### 9.4 Transport Allowance

Employees living more than 15 km from the hospital receive a monthly transport allowance of LKR 8,000. Night-shift staff have access to the hospital shuttle service (routes: Colombo Fort, Nugegoda, Maharagama, Kaduwela).

## 10. Separation & Exit

### 10.1 Resignation

A minimum notice period of 30 calendar days is required. Consultants must provide 90 days' notice. Failure to serve the notice period may result in forfeiture of terminal benefits.

### 10.2 Exit Process

1. Submit resignation letter to HR via the portal.
2. Complete the clearance form (IT equipment, ID badge, library books, locker keys).
3. Attend the exit interview with the HR Business Partner.
4. Final settlement is processed within 14 working days of the last day.
