# Nawaloka Hospital — Cardiology Department Handbook

## 1. Department Overview

The Nawaloka Cardiology Centre is the premier cardiac care facility in Sri Lanka, operating from the 5th and 6th floors of the main hospital building. The department handles over 12,000 outpatient consultations and 3,500 inpatient admissions annually.

### 1.1 Units

- **Cardiac Catheterisation Laboratory (Cath Lab)** — 2 suites (Philips Azurion 7 systems), operational 08:00–20:00 weekdays and 08:00–14:00 Saturdays. Emergency PCI available 24/7 via on-call team.
- **Coronary Care Unit (CCU)** — 12 monitored beds, nurse-to-patient ratio 1:2, continuous telemetry.
- **Cardiac Surgery Unit** — 2 operating theatres dedicated to CABG, valve surgery, and congenital repairs.
- **Cardiac Rehabilitation Centre** — Phase II and III programmes, exercise lab with 6 treadmills and 4 cycle ergometers.
- **Non-Invasive Cardiology Lab** — ECG, Echocardiography (2D, 3D, TEE, stress echo), Holter monitoring, ambulatory BP monitoring.

### 1.2 Consultant Staff

The department has 8 consultant cardiologists, 3 cardiothoracic surgeons, and 2 electrophysiologists. Channelling hours vary by consultant; the schedule is published weekly on the HIS and the hospital website.

## 2. Common Investigations

### 2.1 Electrocardiogram (ECG)

- **Standard 12-lead ECG**: Available at the bedside and in the Non-Invasive Lab. Turnaround time: immediate. Interpretation by the attending MO; consultant review for complex findings.
- **Holter Monitoring (24–72 hours)**: Appointment booked via the Cardiology OPD clerk. The device is fitted in the morning and returned the next day. Report available within 48 hours.
- **Exercise Stress Test (Treadmill Test / TMT)**: Performed using the Bruce protocol. Requires prior consultant referral. The patient must fast for 2 hours and wear comfortable clothing. Contraindications: acute MI within 48 hours, unstable angina, severe aortic stenosis, uncontrolled heart failure.

### 2.3 Cardiac Catheterisation & Angiography

Indications: suspected coronary artery disease, pre-surgical assessment, acute STEMI (primary PCI).

Pre-procedure checklist:
1. Written consent (Form CARD-01).
2. Baseline bloods: FBC, renal function (eGFR), coagulation profile.
3. Allergy status confirmed (especially iodine / contrast allergy — premedication with hydrocortisone and chlorphenamine if history positive).
4. IV access established (18G cannula, right hand preferred).
5. Groin or radial site prepared and marked.
6. NPO for 4 hours prior (clear fluids permitted up to 2 hours before).

Post-procedure care:
- Radial access: TR Band applied for 2–3 hours; patient may mobilise after 2 hours.
- Femoral access: Manual pressure / closure device; bed rest for 4–6 hours with the affected leg kept straight. Check distal pulses (dorsalis pedis, posterior tibial) every 15 minutes for the first hour, then hourly.
- Monitor for complications: bleeding, haematoma, pseudoaneurysm, contrast-induced nephropathy (CIN). IV hydration (N/S at 1 mL/kg/hr) for 12 hours post-procedure to prevent CIN.

## 3. Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) Pathway

### 3.1 STEMI Protocol (Door-to-Balloon ≤ 90 minutes)

1. ED recognises STEMI on the 12-lead ECG.
2. Activate the **STEMI Alert** — Cath Lab team paged (ext. 5555).
3. Administer: Aspirin 300 mg PO + Ticagrelor 180 mg PO (or Clopidogrel 600 mg if ticagrelor unavailable) + Heparin 5,000 IU IV bolus.
4. Transfer to Cath Lab directly (bypass CCU).
5. Primary PCI performed by the interventional cardiologist.
6. Post-PCI → CCU for 24–48 hours minimum.

Target: Door-to-balloon time ≤ 90 minutes. Current hospital median: 72 minutes.

### 3.2 NSTEMI / Unstable Angina Pathway

1. Admit to CCU.
2. Risk stratify using the GRACE Score.
3. Start: dual antiplatelet therapy, anticoagulation (enoxaparin or fondaparinux), beta-blocker, statin, GTN infusion if ongoing pain.
4. Invasive strategy (angiography ± PCI) within 24 hours for high-risk patients (GRACE > 140), within 72 hours for intermediate-risk.

## 4. Heart Failure Management Programme

### 4.1 Inpatient Pathway

- Confirm diagnosis: BNP or NT-proBNP, echocardiography (LVEF assessment).
- Classify: HFrEF (EF ≤ 40%), HFmrEF (EF 41–49%), HFpEF (EF ≥ 50%).
- Initiate guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT): ACE inhibitor/ARB/ARNI + beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2 inhibitor.
- Daily weight, strict fluid balance (target net negative 1–2 L/day in fluid-overloaded patients).
- Diuretic adjustment based on clinical response and renal function.
- Cardiology dietician consult: sodium restriction (< 2 g/day), fluid restriction (1.5 L/day if hyponatraemic).

### 4.2 Heart Failure Clinic (Outpatient)

- Held every Tuesday and Thursday, 09:00–13:00, Cardiology OPD Room 3.
- Managed by the Heart Failure Nurse Specialist (HFNS) and a consultant cardiologist.
- Services: medication titration, device checks (pacemaker/ICD/CRT), patient education, exercise prescription.
- Telephone follow-up at 48 hours post-discharge, clinic visit within 7 days.

## 5. Cardiac Rehabilitation Programme

### 5.1 Phases

| Phase | Setting | Duration | Activities |
|-------|---------|----------|------------|
| I | Inpatient (CCU/Ward) | During admission | Mobilisation, education, psychological support |
| II | Hospital outpatient | 8 weeks (2 sessions/week) | Supervised exercise, lifestyle modification, stress management |
| III | Community / Home | Ongoing | Independent exercise programme, annual review |

### 5.2 Eligibility

Post-MI, post-PCI, post-CABG, stable heart failure (NYHA II–III), post valve surgery, stable angina. Patients are referred by their cardiologist. The rehab team (physiotherapist, dietician, psychologist, exercise physiologist) develops an individualised plan.

### 5.3 Exercise Prescription

- Target heart rate: 60–80% of peak HR achieved on the baseline exercise test.
- RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion): 11–14 on the Borg Scale.
- Duration: 30–45 minutes of aerobic exercise, preceded by 10-minute warm-up and followed by 10-minute cool-down.
- Resistance training: Light weights (1–3 kg), 2 sets of 10 repetitions, major muscle groups, introduced from week 3.

## 6. Device Clinic

The Electrophysiology team runs a Device Clinic every Monday and Wednesday, 08:00–12:00:

- **Pacemaker checks**: Interrogation using manufacturer-specific programmers (Medtronic, Abbott, Boston Scientific). Battery longevity, lead impedance, threshold, and sensing are assessed.
- **ICD checks**: As above, plus review of stored arrhythmia episodes. Patients carry a Device ID Card and must present it at every hospital visit.
- **CRT optimisation**: AV and VV delay programming guided by echocardiographic assessment.
- **Remote monitoring**: Eligible patients are enrolled in home monitoring (CareLink / Merlin.net). Transmissions are reviewed within 2 business days by the device technician; urgent alerts trigger a same-day callback.
