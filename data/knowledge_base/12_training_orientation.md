# Nawaloka Hospital — Staff Training & Orientation Programme

## 1. New Employee Orientation

### 1.1 Orientation Schedule

All new employees attend a **3-day Hospital Orientation Programme** conducted by the HR & Training Department on the first Monday of each month.

| Day | Topics | Facilitator |
|-----|--------|------------|
| Day 1 (Mon) | Welcome address, hospital history & vision, organisational structure, HR policies (leave, payroll, benefits), ID badge & access card activation | HR Manager, IT Support |
| Day 2 (Tue) | Infection control & hand hygiene, fire safety & evacuation drill, patient safety & incident reporting, data privacy & information security | Infection Control Nurse, Fire Safety Officer, Quality Manager, Privacy Officer |
| Day 3 (Wed) | HIS (Hospital Information System) training, department-specific induction, shadowing with a buddy/mentor, tour of key areas (pharmacy, lab, ED, canteen) | IT Trainer, Department Head, Assigned Buddy |

### 1.2 Clinical Staff Additional Requirements

Clinical staff (doctors, nurses, allied health) must complete the following within the first 30 days:

1. **BLS (Basic Life Support) Certification** — 4-hour course, conducted weekly by the Resuscitation Training Unit. Valid for 2 years.
2. **Blood Transfusion Competency** — Online module + practical assessment.
3. **Medication Safety Module** — Online, 45 minutes, with quiz (passing score ≥ 80%).
4. **Manual Handling Training** — 2-hour practical session covering patient lifting, transfers, and ergonomic techniques.
5. **Restraint Training** (for ED, psychiatry, ICU staff) — 3-hour session on de-escalation techniques and safe use of physical restraints.

### 1.3 Buddy/Mentor Programme

Every new employee is paired with an experienced colleague (minimum 2 years' service) for the first 3 months. The buddy provides informal guidance, answers questions, and facilitates social integration. The HR department checks in with both the new hire and the buddy at 1 month and 3 months.

## 2. Mandatory Annual Training

All staff must complete the following training annually. Completion is tracked via the Learning Management System (LMS) and is linked to the performance appraisal.

| Training Module | Format | Duration | Deadline |
|----------------|--------|----------|----------|
| Hand Hygiene & Infection Control Update | Online + practical assessment | 1 hour | March |
| Fire Safety & Evacuation Drill | In-person drill | 30 min | Quarterly (any one) |
| Data Privacy & Information Security | Online (e-learning) | 1 hour | June |
| Patient Safety & Incident Reporting | Online | 45 min | September |
| Code of Conduct & Anti-Harassment | Online | 30 min | December |
| BLS Re-certification (clinical staff) | In-person | 4 hours | Every 2 years |
| ACLS Re-certification (ED, ICU, anaesthesia, cardiology) | In-person | 8 hours | Every 2 years |

Staff who do not complete mandatory training by the deadline receive a written reminder. Persistent non-compliance is escalated to the Department Head and may affect performance ratings.

## 3. Continuing Medical Education (CME)

### 3.1 CME Requirements

The Sri Lanka Medical Council (SLMC) requires all registered medical practitioners to accumulate **30 CME credits per year** to maintain their practising certificate.

Nawaloka supports CME through:
- **In-house CME sessions**: Weekly Grand Rounds (Fridays, 13:00–14:00, Auditorium), departmental case presentations, morbidity & mortality conferences.
- **Conference sponsorship**: Up to LKR 150,000 per consultant per year for approved local conferences; up to LKR 500,000 for international conferences (with a presentation or poster commitment).
- **Journal Club**: Monthly, facilitated by each department on a rotating basis.
- **Online resources**: UpToDate, BMJ Best Practice, and NEJM Knowledge+ subscriptions available via the Medical Library portal (accessible on and off campus).

### 3.2 CME Credit Allocation

| Activity | Credits |
|----------|---------|
| Attending Grand Rounds (1 hour) | 1 credit |
| Presenting at Grand Rounds | 3 credits |
| Departmental case presentation (1 hour) | 1 credit |
| Attending an approved conference (per day) | 6 credits |
| Publishing a peer-reviewed paper | 10 credits |
| Completing an online CME module (accredited) | Credits as per accreditor |
| Supervising a trainee (per rotation) | 5 credits |

### 3.3 CPD for Nurses and Allied Health

Registered nurses must accumulate **20 Continuing Professional Development (CPD) points per year** as required by the Sri Lanka Nursing Council. Allied health professionals follow their respective professional council requirements.

Nawaloka's Nursing Education Unit conducts monthly in-service training sessions covering: wound care, IV therapy updates, paediatric assessment, geriatric care, palliative care, and clinical leadership.

## 4. Specialist Training Programmes

### 4.1 Nursing Specialisation

Nawaloka offers post-registration specialist courses in partnership with the University of Colombo and the Post-Basic Nursing School:

| Programme | Duration | Eligibility |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Critical Care Nursing | 6 months | RN with ≥ 2 years ICU/CCU experience |
| Perioperative Nursing | 6 months | RN with ≥ 1 year theatre experience |
| Oncology Nursing | 6 months | RN with ≥ 1 year oncology ward experience |
| Neonatal Nursing | 6 months | RN with ≥ 1 year NICU experience |
| Emergency Nursing | 6 months | RN with ≥ 1 year ED experience |

Nawaloka sponsors the course fees for selected candidates (bond: 2-year service commitment post-completion).

### 4.2 Medical Trainee Programmes

Nawaloka is an accredited training centre for the Postgraduate Institute of Medicine (PGIM) in the following specialties:
- Internal Medicine (MD)
- Surgery (MS)
- Paediatrics (MD)
- Obstetrics & Gynaecology (MD)
- Anaesthesiology (MD)
- Cardiology (Board Certification)
- Radiology (MD)

Training programmes follow PGIM curricula with dedicated clinical supervisors, logbook requirements, and regular assessments.

### 4.3 Leadership Development Programme

The Nawaloka Leadership Academy (NLA) offers an annual 6-month programme for high-potential managers and senior clinicians covering:
- Strategic thinking and decision-making
- Healthcare quality and patient safety leadership
- Financial literacy for non-finance managers
- Communication and conflict resolution
- Change management in healthcare
- Project management (Lean Six Sigma Yellow Belt)

Graduates of the NLA programme are prioritised for internal promotions and committee appointments.

## 5. Simulation Training Centre

The Nawaloka Simulation Lab (3rd Floor, Education Wing) features:

- **High-fidelity mannequins**: Adult (Laerdal SimMan 3G Plus), paediatric (SimJunior), and neonatal (SimNewB) for realistic clinical scenario training.
- **Task trainers**: IV insertion arms, intubation heads, suturing pads, central line insertion torsos, lumbar puncture trainers.
- **Virtual reality (VR) modules**: Laparoscopic surgery simulation, endoscopy simulation.
- **Standardised patient programme**: Trained actors for communication skills training, breaking bad news, and history-taking assessments.

Simulation sessions are scheduled by the Simulation Coordinator and are available to all clinical departments. Each department is allocated 4 hours per month. The simulation lab operates Monday–Friday, 08:00–17:00.

## 6. Competency Assessment

### 6.1 Annual Competency Checks

All clinical staff undergo annual competency assessments relevant to their role:

| Role | Key Competencies Assessed |
|------|--------------------------|
| Registered Nurse | IV cannulation, medication administration, blood transfusion, ECG interpretation, BLS, patient assessment |
| Medical Officer | ACLS, procedural skills (lumbar puncture, chest drain, central line), clinical reasoning |
| Radiographer | Radiation safety, positioning, image quality, contrast administration |
| Lab Technologist | Specimen handling, analyser operation, QC interpretation, critical value reporting |
| Pharmacist | Prescription review, drug interaction identification, patient counselling, sterile compounding |

### 6.2 Remediation

Staff who do not meet the competency standard receive a remediation plan:
1. Supervised practice sessions with a clinical educator.
2. Re-assessment within 30 days.
3. If still below standard, reassignment to supervised duties until competence is achieved.
