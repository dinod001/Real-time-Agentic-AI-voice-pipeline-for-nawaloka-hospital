## 2. Test Ordering & Specimen Collection

### 2.1 Electronic Ordering

All tests are ordered through the HIS (Lab Order Entry). Paper request forms are accepted only during system downtime or for external referrals. Every order generates a unique Lab Accession Number (LAN) and a barcode label.

### 2.2 Specimen Collection Guidelines

#### Blood Specimens

| Tube Colour | Additive | Common Tests |
|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Red (plain) | None (clot activator) | Serum chemistry, serology, drug levels |
| Lavender (EDTA) | K₂EDTA | FBC, HbA1c, blood group, reticulocyte count |
| Light Blue (citrate) | 3.2% sodium citrate | PT/INR, APTT, D-dimer, fibrinogen |
| Green (lithium heparin) | Lithium heparin | Electrolytes (STAT), ammonia |
| Grey (fluoride) | Sodium fluoride / potassium oxalate | Glucose (fasting, random, OGTT) |
| Yellow (ACD) | Acid-citrate-dextrose | Blood bank cross-match, HLA typing |
| Royal Blue (trace element) | EDTA or no additive | Heavy metals, trace elements |

**Order of draw** (to prevent additive contamination): Blood culture → Citrate → Plain → Heparin → EDTA → Fluoride.

### 2.3 Specimen Labelling

Every specimen must be labelled at the **bedside** immediately after collection with:
- Patient full name
- Hospital Number (HN)
- Date and time of collection
- Collector's initials

Unlabelled or mislabelled specimens are **rejected** — a new sample must be drawn.

### 2.4 Specimen Transport

- **Pneumatic Tube System (PTS)**: Connects ED, ICU, CCU, OT, and wards to the central lab. Specimens are placed in sealed capsules. Fragile specimens (blood cultures, CSF, synovial fluid) must **not** be sent via PTS — hand-delivered by porters.
- **Cold Chain**: Specimens requiring refrigeration (e.g., ammonia, ACTH, renin) are transported in insulated bags with gel ice packs. Target: arrival in the lab within 30 minutes of collection.

## 4. Critical Value Notification

Critical values are results that require immediate clinical attention. The laboratory technologist must notify the treating physician or ward nurse **within 30 minutes** of result verification.

### 4.1 Critical Value List

| Analyte | Low Critical | High Critical |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Haemoglobin | < 7.0 g/dL | > 20.0 g/dL |
| Platelets | < 50,000/µL | > 1,000,000/µL |
| WBC | < 2,000/µL | > 30,000/µL |
| Potassium | < 2.5 mmol/L | > 6.5 mmol/L |
| Sodium | < 120 mmol/L | > 160 mmol/L |
| Glucose | < 2.5 mmol/L (45 mg/dL) | > 25 mmol/L (450 mg/dL) |
| Creatinine | — | > 700 µmol/L |
| Troponin I | — | > 0.04 ng/mL (above 99th percentile) |
| INR | — | > 5.0 |
| Calcium (corrected) | < 1.6 mmol/L | > 3.5 mmol/L |
| Lactate | — | > 4.0 mmol/L |
| Blood culture | Positive (any organism) | — |

The notifying technologist documents: result, date/time, name and designation of the person notified, and read-back confirmation.

## 5. Quality Control & Assurance

### 5.1 Internal Quality Control (IQC)

- Two levels of QC material (normal and abnormal) are run at the start of each shift and after calibration.
- Results are evaluated using Westgard rules (1₂ₛ, 2₂ₛ, R₄ₛ, 1₃ₛ, 4₁ₛ, 10x̄).
- Out-of-control results trigger investigation before patient results are released.

### 5.2 External Quality Assessment (EQA)

The laboratory participates in EQA schemes:
- Randox International Quality Assessment Scheme (RIQAS) — Chemistry, Haematology.
- UK NEQAS — Microbiology, Histopathology.
- College of American Pathologists (CAP) — Immunoassay.

EQA results are reviewed monthly by the Quality Manager. Persistent outliers trigger corrective action.

## 6. Blood Bank

### 6.1 Blood Component Availability

| Component | Storage Conditions | Shelf Life |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Packed Red Blood Cells (PRBC) | 2–6°C | 35 days (CPDA-1) |
| Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP) | ≤ -25°C | 12 months |
| Platelet Concentrate | 20–24°C with agitation | 5 days |
| Cryoprecipitate | ≤ -25°C | 12 months |
| Whole Blood | 2–6°C | 35 days |

### 6.2 Compatibility Testing

- **Type & Screen**: ABO/Rh typing + antibody screening. Valid for 72 hours.
- **Cross-Match**: Electronic cross-match for patients with a negative antibody screen; serological cross-match (immediate spin + AHG phase) for patients with positive antibody screens or a history of transfusion reactions.
- **Emergency Release**: Group O Rh-negative PRBCs may be issued without cross-match in life-threatening emergencies. The Blood Bank Consultant must be notified immediately.

### 6.3 Massive Transfusion Protocol (MTP)

Activated when ≥ 10 units of PRBCs are anticipated within 24 hours (e.g., major trauma, obstetric haemorrhage):
- Component ratio: 1:1:1 (PRBC : FFP : Platelet).
- The blood bank prepares a "MTP Pack" (6 PRBC + 6 FFP + 1 apheresis platelet) and replenishes as needed.
- Calcium gluconate 10% is administered after every 4 units to counteract citrate toxicity.
